Life Insurance Pricing Model project

Link to full project - https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/15qFrDGDchRUf2r1a_5xfxE8dqtwWEUt5av2AuEN6TaA/edit?gid=1646675705#gid=1646675705

Data from Social Security Administration - https://www.ssa.gov/oact/STATS/table4c6.html

A life table is a tool used to display a summary of mortality and surival rates for a population. It calculates the probability a person of a certain age will die or survive and can show expected future life expectancy. This is a life table I created based on the Social Security Administration's data on the US population separated by male and female. I included a mortality adjustment factor that can be used to manipulate the mortality rate of the population and compare the results. Using these inputs, I developed a 20 year life insurance pricing model that projects year-by-year expected death benefits, discounts them to present value, and derives a net single premium, net level annual premium, and gross annual premium. The model is fully dynamic, allowing scenario analysis across a range of customer profiles.
